Meet Jane

Jane has always put money aside each, month for that 'rainy day'. She's worked hard for it and knowing that she has a little bit extra stashed away means she can treat herself now and again ... Like the new patio she's just had put in. After all, she'll be spending a lot more time in the garden now she's retired -hopefully without too many rainy days!

Jane chose to take the maximum tax-free cash lump sum, to top up her treat fund, and used what was left to buy an annuity. This will give her peace of mind and a regular income for the rest of her life.


Read the full factsheet

Jane Prioritized...

Having other sources income
Jane wasn't relying on the money from his DC savings to pay for life after work.

Flexibility
Jane wanted to use his DC fund to pay for a few 'little extras'.

Short-term income
Jane knew that his DC fund wouldn't last long or would only pay a small regular amount as an annuity, so he decided to take it all upfront.

Meet Anouska

Proud mother of three boys and soon to be grandmother for the first time. With her eldest about to have his first child and her youngest about to get married and buy a house, Anouska is looking forward to spending more time with her growing family during retirement.

Anouska decided to invest her pension savings into a Drawdown fund so she can help out her family with some of life's larger expenses.


Read the full factsheet

Anouska Prioritized...

Opportunity
Anouska hoped that by keeping her savings invested she could grow them for the future.

Flexibility
She liked being able to withdraw money as and when she needed; particularly for family events. She also knew that she could pass funds on to the next generation when the time came.

Short-term income
She took the maximum-tax free lump sum upfront to give her children a helping hand.

Meet Girish

Girish always knew he wouldn't stay in this job for long. Having worked for 30 years at his previous employer he'd built up a large defined benefit pension. But when he'd been 1nade redundant unexpectedly a couple of years ago Girish had just a little more time to go before he could retire. Now that day had come and he could start receiving his pension.

With Girish's other pension providing sufficient income in retirement and only a small DC fund, Girish decided to take his DC fund as a single cash lump sum, even though he knew he'd pay tax on 75% of it.
