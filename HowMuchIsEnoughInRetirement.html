HOW MUCH IS ENOUGH IN RETIREMENT

  1. Home
  2. How Much Is Enough In Retirement

How much is enough?

Whether it's enjoying more quality time with friends and family or doing something ambitious like travelling the world, retirement can be a fulfilling and rewarding time of life. But only if you save enough!

You need a clear target so you don't find yourself short of money later on in life. £14,167.40 is currently what the Office of the National Statistics says will provide the minimum decent standard of living in retirement. But you'll probably want more than this.

How much do you think you'll need?

The State pension should cover some of the basics in retirement like housing, utility bills and groceries, but it may not cover other essentials like clothes, travel costs and mobile phone.

Your DHL pension is a great way to save for these essentials and the little extras you might want in retirement.


When you retire you may have 4 sources of income

State pension DHL pension Previous pension Other State benefits, income and savings
£8,767.20 max a year per person

The amount of State pension you'll recieve will depend on your National insurance contributions.

Check your State pension forecast at gov.uk

 The forecast of your DHL pension (on the App in your online account) gives you an estimate of the income you might get each year in retirement. You may have benefits from another pension plans. You may be entitled to other State benefits, choose to take part-time job or use other savings to boost your income.

Do the maths

Once you've got a figure in mind, you need to workout how much you'll need to save over your working life. Let's say you want £20,000 a year and you'll retire at age 67. If you get the maximum State pension, you'll need around £450,000 of savings to provide the remaining £11,500 a year.

It sounds a lot, but remember DHL pays in too and over time investment returns can help your savings grow.

Did you know?

Currently, you can take your savings from the Plan any time from age 55.
